About this website:

This website is a prototype, and it's still WIP.

If it ever gets developed into a real thing it will be a place to host coding challenges for programmers.

Unlike most corporate hackatons, this is not a business case. It's actually about programming.

Unlike traditional hackatons this website relies on a homemade engine that supports a "game-like" graphical interface, in which the effects of your code can be seen in real-time.

The rules are simple:
- The language is Javascript. While you can write all the code you want, your task is to fill in the update() function.
- update() has one parameter, the state: an object containing everything you need to know about the simulated environment and all the actions that can be performed to affect it.
- update() will be called three times a second.

Challenge 0: Tutorial

This is mostly to get everyone familiarized with the system. The simulated "world" is a 10x10 grid, with the origin on the bottom-left. The state contains two objects:
- target, with an attribute position
- player, also with a position, but also exposing a move() function.

Get player to the target.

Challenge 1: Groceries

We start introducing more objects to the environment: a shopping trolley.
The shopping trolley can be pushed by the player by moving into it.

Push the trolley to the target.

Challenge 2: Sheep Dog

Once again, the goal here is to move in a way that makes something else move to the target.
This time it's a herd of sheep.

Differently from a shopping cart, they have a mind on their own. They do move away from you, but in an impredictable manner.
Sometimes they even move completely on their own when you're far away!
